Caution

Intense exercise should be intense but brief. Don't exceed your fitness level and use medically informed judgment regarding the degree and duration of exercise, especially in conjunction with exposure to cold temperature.

Background

Our heightened emotions are a survival weapon, such as anger preparing us to fight, or fear to run away. It can be difficult to avoid action altogether, so instead of acting on our emotional urges, intense exercise can reduce our emotional intensity and give us back control. This can be helpful in situations with high emotional distress, panic, or when feeling overwhelmed, anxiety interfering with tasks requiring concentration, inability to sleep, dissociation, anger, or other very intense emotions.

Instructions
Once the exercise stops, the parasympathetic nervous system is activated, reducing emotional intensity for about 20 minutes. Try one or a combination of the aerobic exercises below. Then take the time to consider what other skills can be used to maintain control.
  • Go for a run around the block
  • Start a high-intensity weight circuit at the gym or at home
  • Do push-ups, sit-ups, jumping jacks, power walking, etc
  • Turn on some music and dance around in your room
  • Jump Rope
